Conversion of Extended Toggles

It is common in extended toggle coverage that not all togglenodes in a given scope are capable of producing Z values.

To address the issue of togglenodes that cannot produce Z values, all modes of extended toggle coverage calculation allow 100% coverage for lh and hl, as long as no Z edge occurs. When an extended togglenode with no Z edges is observed in simulation, it is implicitly converted to a 2-state togglenode.

The 2-state togglenode has only0L->1H and 1H->0L counts. Such togglenodes have their mode of extended toggle coverage reported as '2-STATE' in coverage reports. If such a togglenode is eventually merged (via vcover merge or similar) with a simulation that contains Z edges, the merge is pessimistic: the merged result contains all the Z edges.
